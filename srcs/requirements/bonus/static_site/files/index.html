Classic Shakshouka

A Middle Eastern breakfast favorite

Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 25 minutes
Servings: 4 p.

Ingredients

  • 6 eggs
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 medium fresh tomatoes, diced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • salt and pepper
  • fresh parsley or cilantro, chopped
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper (optional)
  • feta cheese, crumbled (optional)
  • pita bread for serving

Instructions

  1. Saut√© the vegetables: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and bell pepper, cook for about 5 minutes until softened.
  2. Add spices: Add garlic, cumin, paprika, and cayenne pepper. Cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add tomatoes: Add in the diced tomatoes and season with salt and pepper. Let it simmer for 10-15 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  4. Make wells for eggs: Using a spoon, create 6 small wells in the tomato sauce.
  5. Add eggs: Crack one egg into each well. Cover the skillet and cook for 5-7 minutes until the egg whites are set but yolks are still runny.
  6. Garnish and serve: Sprinkle with fresh herbs and feta cheese if desired. Serve hot with warm pita bread.

Tips

  • For a spicier version, add more cayenne or fresh chilli peppers
  • You can add spinach or other greens to the tomato sauce
  • Best served family-style, straight from the pan
  • Leftovers reheat well for a quick breakfast
